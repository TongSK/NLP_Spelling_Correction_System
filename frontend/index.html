    Non-word Detection

    Words absent from the corpus vocabulary are flagged. SymSpell generates candidates in O(1) using a pre-computed delete-neighbourhood index.

    Real-word Detection

    Context-sensitive errors are detected by BERT (bert-base-uncased). If the masked log-probability of the word is low and a confusable alternative scores higher, the word is flagged.

    BERT (Masked LM)

    The word is replaced with [MASK] and BERT predicts the most likely token. This gives true bidirectional context — far beyond what bigrams can capture.

    SymSpell

    Pre-computes all deletes within edit distance 2 at load time. Lookup is O(1) average vs O(n·|vocab|) for brute-force Levenshtein.

    Minimum Edit Distance

    Levenshtein distance (insertions, deletions, substitutions) annotates every suggestion chip so users see exactly how far the correction is from the original word.

    Noisy Channel Model

    Final score = log P(candidate) + 0.5 × bigram score + 1.0 × BERT score. Combines corpus prior with contextual evidence.

    Bigram Language Model

    Add-k smoothed bigram counts from the corpus serve as a fast fallback when BERT is unavailable, and as an additional signal during re-ranking.

    Corpus

    Medical science domain — Gray's Anatomy, Merck Manual, Materia Medica, and supplementary NLTK corpora — totalling 100 000+ word tokens.
