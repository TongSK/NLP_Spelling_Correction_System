    Non-word Detection

    Words absent from the corpus vocabulary are flagged as non-word errors. Candidates are ranked by minimum edit distance (Levenshtein) combined with unigram probability from the corpus.

    Real-word Detection

    Context-sensitive errors are detected using bigram language model probabilities. If P(wi|wi-1) is significantly lower than a plausible alternative, the word is flagged.

    Minimum Edit Distance (Levenshtein)

    Dynamic programming computes the minimum number of insertions, deletions, and substitutions to transform one word into another. Candidates with distance ≤ 2 are presented.

    Noisy Channel Model

    Best correction = argmax P(word) × P(typo|word). Combines the prior probability of a word (language model) with the likelihood of producing the observed error.

    Bigram Language Model

    Probabilities P(wi|wi-1) are estimated from the medical corpus. Used both for real-word error detection and for re-ranking correction candidates by context.

    Corpus Details

    Medical science domain (~100,000+ word tokens). Vocabulary covers clinical text including pharmacology, pathology, anatomy, immunology, and diagnostic terminology.
